Matteo Della Rossa

 

Assistant Professor (RTD-B, fixed-term, tenure-track) within the Automatica Research Group in DET (Dipartimento di Elettronica e Telecomunicazioni), Politecnico di Torino (Turin, Italy).

Research Interests

    My research interests include but are not limited to the following:

  • Hybrid (switched) dynamical systems: Stability and stabilization;

  • Non-smooth analysis and non-smooth Lyapunov functions;

  • Control applications of graphs and automata theory;

  • Optimal control for hybrid/delay systems.

(Very) Short Bio

I was born in 1992 in Gemona del Friuli (UD), in the north-eastern region of Friuli, Italy. I received my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Mathematics from the University of Udine, my hometown, in 2015 and 2017, respectively. I then moved to Toulouse, France, where I pursued a Ph.D. in Automatic Control at the LAAS-CNRS laboratory, which I obtained in 2020. From November 2020 to May 2023, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Université catholique de Louvain (Louvain-la-Neuve, Belgium). From May 2023 to June 2025, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher (Assegnista di Ricerca) at the University of Udine (Udine, Italy). In September 2025, I moved to Turin, where I am currently based.
